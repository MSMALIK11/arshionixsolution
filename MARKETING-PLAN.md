# Arshionix — Marketing plan to get clients

A practical plan to drive traffic and turn visitors into clients. Pick 2–3 channels first, do them well, then add more.

---

## Part 1: On-site (your website)

**Goal:** Turn visitors into leads.

| Action | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **Clear CTA everywhere** | You already have “Get a free quote” and “Book a call”. Keep them above the fold and in the footer. |
| **Trust signals** | Add logos (e.g. “Trusted by X companies”), 1–2 short testimonials with name/photo, and a line like “50+ projects delivered” if true. |
| **Lead magnet** | Offer something useful in exchange for email: e.g. “Free checklist: 10 things before you build an app” or “Web project cost guide (PDF)”. Capture email → nurture with 2–3 emails, then offer a call. |
| **Blog / Insights** | 1–2 posts per month on topics like “How much does a custom web app cost?”, “Web vs mobile: what to build first?”. Use your main keywords (see SEO-GUIDE.md). Helps SEO and shows expertise. |
| **Case studies** | When you have 2–3 good projects: add a “Work” or “Case studies” page with problem, solution, result, and a CTA. |

---

## Part 2: Platforms to use (priority order)

### Tier 1 — Start here (highest impact for agencies)

| Platform | What to do | Why |
|----------|------------|-----|
| **LinkedIn** | Create a **Company Page** for Arshionix. Post 2–3x/week: tips (web, Android, UI/UX), short project wins, “what we’re building”, culture. Connect with founders, product managers, startup people. Comment on their posts. Share your posts from your personal profile. | Best for B2B and decision-makers. |
| **Google Business Profile** | Create a profile for “Arshionix” with website, location (or service area), phone, hours. Add a few photos and a short description. Ask happy clients for Google reviews. | Shows up in “digital agency near me” and brand search. |
| **Clutch / GoodFirms** | Create company profiles and add services, location, portfolio. Ask past clients for reviews. | Many clients shortlist agencies from these directories. |

### Tier 2 — Add after Tier 1 is running

| Platform | What to do |
|----------|------------|
| **Twitter/X** | Follow people in startups, product, tech. Share short tips, links to blog posts, project snippets. Reply and quote-tweet. |
| **YouTube** | Short “how we built X” or “cost of Y” videos. Embed on site and share on LinkedIn/Twitter. |
| **Reddit / Quora** | Answer questions in r/startups, r/webdev, r/Entrepreneur, Quora (“how to hire a dev agency”, “web app cost”). Be helpful, add a soft link to your site in bio or when relevant. |
| **Indie Hackers / Product Hunt** | Engage in IH; launch your own product or “Arshionix” as a brand when you have something to show. |

### Tier 3 — When you have content and time

| Platform | What to do |
|----------|------------|
| **Instagram** | Behind-the-scenes, team, design snippets, office/culture. Good for brand and recruitment. |
| **Facebook** | Company page + optional small ad budget to target “business owners” or “founders” in your region. |
| **Newsletter** | Once you have a blog + lead magnet, send a monthly “Arshionix insights” with 1–2 tips and a CTA. |

---

## Part 3: 90-day plan (example)

### Month 1 — Foundation
- [ ] LinkedIn Company Page live; 3–5 posts.
- [ ] Google Business Profile created and verified.
- [ ] Clutch and/or GoodFirms profile filled.
- [ ] One lead magnet on the site (PDF or checklist) + simple “Subscribe” or “Download” form.
- [ ] Ask 2–3 past clients for a short testimonial and a review (Google + Clutch).

### Month 2 — Content + consistency
- [ ] Publish 2 blog posts (use keywords from SEO-GUIDE).
- [ ] LinkedIn: 2–3 posts per week; start commenting on 5–10 relevant posts per week.
- [ ] Add testimonials and “Trusted by” (or “X projects delivered”) on homepage.
- [ ] Optional: run a small Google or LinkedIn ad (e.g. “web development agency”) to test.

### Month 3 — Scale what works
- [ ] Double down on the channel that brought the most traffic or leads (e.g. LinkedIn or Google).
- [ ] First simple case study on the site.
- [ ] Start Twitter or YouTube if you have capacity.
- [ ] Repeat: more blog posts, more LinkedIn, more reviews.

---

## Part 4: Quick reference — where to focus

| Goal | Main action |
|------|-------------|
| **More visitors** | SEO (blog + keywords), Google Business, LinkedIn posts, directory profiles. |
| **More leads** | Strong CTAs, lead magnet, testimonials, “Book a call” / “Get a quote”. |
| **More trust** | Testimonials, case studies, Clutch/GoodFirms reviews, Google reviews. |
| **Reach decision-makers** | LinkedIn (personal + company), Clutch, niche communities (Reddit, IH). |

---

## Summary

1. **On-site:** CTAs, trust (testimonials, numbers), one lead magnet, then blog + case studies.
2. **Platforms (in order):** LinkedIn → Google Business → Clutch/GoodFirms → then Twitter, YouTube, Reddit/Quora.
3. **90 days:** Set up profiles (LinkedIn, Google, Clutch), add testimonials and lead magnet, post regularly, then add content and paid tests.

Stick to 2–3 channels at first. Consistency beats spreading too thin.
